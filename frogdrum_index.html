The Frog Drum

The frog drum is one of the Karen's ancient traditional instruments. In the past, people often played it to gather crowd for works, wars, meetings or celebrations. Today, people play it only for special occasions such as weddings, funerals, Wrist Tying Ceremony, or Karen New Year. As the frog drum is one of the most significant signature of Karen people, it showcases the key symbol of Karen culture on the flag. The frogs on the drum also represents one behavior of Karen people which is a desire to be calm, nonchalant, and peaceful.
